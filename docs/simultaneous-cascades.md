# Simultaneous Cascades Guide

This guide explains how to effectively use the Simultaneous Cascades feature introduced in Windsurf Wave 9.

## What are Simultaneous Cascades?

Simultaneous Cascades allow you to run multiple Cascade conversations concurrently, enabling you to work on different tasks without waiting for one conversation to complete before starting another.

## Key Benefits

- **Increased Productivity**: Work on multiple tasks simultaneously
- **Improved Workflow**: Start a new conversation while waiting for a long-running task to complete
- **Better Context Management**: Separate different concerns into different conversations
- **Enhanced Collaboration**: Run different conversations for different team members or purposes

## How to Use Simultaneous Cascades

### Starting a New Cascade

1. While a Cascade is already running, click the "+" button in the Cascade panel
2. This will open a new Cascade conversation tab
3. You can now interact with this new Cascade independently

### Switching Between Cascades

1. Click on the Cascade tabs at the top of the Cascade panel
2. Each tab represents a separate conversation
3. You can freely switch between them without losing context

### Notifications

When a background Cascade requires your attention (e.g., it has completed a task or is waiting for input), you'll receive a notification:

1. A badge will appear on the Cascade tab
2. A notification will appear in the Windsurf notification center
3. (Optional) A desktop notification if enabled in settings

## Advanced Features

### Naming Cascades

You can name your Cascades for better organization:

1. Right-click on a Cascade tab
2. Select "Rename"
3. Enter a descriptive name for the Cascade

### Sharing Cascades

You can share a specific Cascade conversation with team members:

1. Click the "Share" button in the Cascade panel
2. Copy the generated link
3. Share it with your team members

### Cascade Management

You can manage your Cascades from the Cascade management panel:

1. Click the "Manage Cascades" button in the Cascade panel
2. View all active Cascades
3. Close, rename, or share Cascades

## Best Practices

1. **Task Separation**: Use different Cascades for different tasks or features
2. **Long-Running Tasks**: Start a new Cascade for quick questions while waiting for a long-running task
3. **Context Switching**: Use different Cascades when switching between different parts of your codebase
4. **Team Collaboration**: Use dedicated Cascades for different team members or roles
5. **Resource Management**: Close Cascades when they're no longer needed to free up resources
